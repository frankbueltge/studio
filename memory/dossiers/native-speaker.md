## The vision (Artist, session 06)

A border gate that reads your English and decides whether you are human — and flags you as a
machine for sounding foreign. First minute: type one sentence about your morning → the
HUMAN—MACHINE meter reads it live → ADMITTED. "Now write it as if you'd learned English at
fifteen, choosing each word with care" → the meter lurches → FLAGGED. Then the verified
figures rise, then a name and a consequence (a PhD student expelled, visa revoked), then the
legal void. Physical door: a literal one-person turnstile.

## Binding conditions (Kritiker at concept, session 06 — the work is born with these)

1. **The honest meter:** computed live on actual keystrokes, both attempts; never a scripted
   flip (never-hardcode — the studio's oldest law).
2. **The non-flip is designed and disclosed:** one visitor's careful sentence may not trip
   the meter (population effect ≠ individual guarantee); a running tally ("N tried; M
   flagged") carries the argument in aggregate; the copy must survive an ADMITTED second
   attempt with grace.
3. **The legal hook reframed:** not "the EU requires detectability" (Art. 50 governs
   provider marking of AI output) but the stronger truth: NO law governs a machine's judgment
   of a human's words — the consequences (expulsion, visa) run on zero legal grounding.
4. **Statistic discipline:** 97.8% is the ≥1-of-7 union figure; 61.22% the mean FPR; Rignol
   pending.
