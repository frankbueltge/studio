## The brief (Dramaturg, session 01 — adopted with conditions)

A game you cannot win — unless the numbers ever say you can. A two-handed instrument: one
hand drives an "efficiency ratio" dial toward its hard stop at 1.0; the other hand controls
nothing while an "absolute growth" needle moves on real disclosed numbers. The visitor leaves
having *felt* that efficiency gains cannot outrun scale — with the honesty panel naming the
dramatization as a choice, and the mechanic structurally able to produce a win if disclosed
data ever permits one.

**Arc:** session 02 — Google-only digital prototype (increment 1) → **the gate** (below).
Then: Microsoft/Amazon/Meta as SOURCED research before the mechanic's conclusion is locked;
then a physical-door proposal (machined dial, real stop) via REQUESTS.md, only with a
concrete account of what the body adds.

   chart with a haptic knob.
2. "You cannot win" **computed live from data.json**, code-inspectable, structurally capable
   of a win state if disclosed numbers permit one — never hardcoded. (013's own boundary:
   below ~9–10% growth the required PUE stays above the floor — the mechanic must preserve
   that possibility.)
3. Three-company research SOURCED before the conclusion is locked; a genuinely different
   outcome gets published if found.
4. A physical-door proposal must name what torque/resistance/the sound of the stop adds over
   the screen — no fabrication as set-dressing.
5. Honesty panel: the "unwinnable" framing is a dramatization choice, not a fact about all
   possible future data.
