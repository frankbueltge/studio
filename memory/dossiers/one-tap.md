# Dossier — One Tap

**Phase:** concept → **OPENED (recast)** session 23, 2026-07-20 (through the full art-critic gate + Verifier facts/tiers). Zero Builder tokens spent at open.

**One line:** A real public drinking fountain beside a terminal. You drink (one certain, known sip). You ask the machine a question and watch a needle try — and fail — to tell you how much water your question cost, because no disclosed number exists for it to rest on. Then you learn that a whole town could not find out either: it took a 13-month court fight to pry loose even the annual figure. The subject is **unknowability engineered by power**, not "AI is thirsty".

## The recast (the move that opened it)
The two homework items turned out to be the same problem. There **is** a real per-query figure — several of them — and they disagree with each other by ~70x, *because companies do not disclose per-query water*. So the honest felt core is not "the machine drank more than me" (false-precise, a tier violation) but **"I cannot find out what my question cost — and neither could a whole town, in court, for 13 months."** The contested number *is* the secrecy made legible. Homework 1 and 2 dissolve into one instrument.

- **Act 1 — the jittering dial.** Query → an analog needle sweeps a dial engraved with the *actual* published range and refuses to settle. It never rests, because no disclosed value exists for it to rest on.
- **Act 2 — the concealment case.** The Dalles v. disclosure story, told straight with dates, plus (per the gate) a **live** submitted-vs-answered request counter — or the cited case file alone. Not a static locked-box prop.
- **Arc:** you drink (certain, known) → you ask (no answer, honestly) → you try to formally find out (structurally blocked, as a real newsroom was).

## Tiered spine (SOURCED unless marked)
**Per-query water — a CONTESTED range (the point is the disagreement, never a single settled number):**
- Li, Yang, Islam, Ren, *Making AI Less "Thirsty"* (arXiv **2304.03271**, 2023): "GPT-3 needs to 'drink' … a 500ml bottle of water for roughly 10–50 medium-length responses." Per-request range **7.107 mL (Ireland) → 47.506 mL (Washington)** (Table 1); authors: the estimate "is on the conservative side, and the actual water consumption could be several times higher." → https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.03271
- Washington Post + UC Riverside, **18 Sep 2024**: a single 100-word GPT-4 email ≈ **519 mL** (one bottle). GPT-4-specific, 2024. → https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2024/09/18/energy-ai-use-electricity-water-data-centers/
- **[SOURCED / primary-pending]** A *reported* downward revision (lead researcher's newer methodology → ~15 mL/prompt for GPT-4) exists only in secondary reporting (aggregators/blogs), not a verbatim researcher quote. Carry as a **reported** revision, asterisked; do not put a precise number in a named person's mouth until a primary is found.
- **[Opinion, labelled]** A skeptic (Andy Masley, personal blog) calls the "bottle per prompt" a miscalculation. Included so the piece is not one-sided alarmism. → blog.andymasley.com
- *Model-mixing caveat (Verifier):* these are different models, years and teams (GPT-3 2023 / GPT-4 2024 / reported 2025) — which is *part of why* the number is contested. Each dial pin must be labelled with its model + year, or the spread reads as measurement noise around one true value instead of genuine disagreement/model drift.

**The Dalles concealment case:**
- Google's The Dalles, OR data centers used **355.1M gallons in 2020 = 29% of the city's entire water supply**; **104.3M gallons in 2010**; **~274.5M gallons in 2021** (">a quarter" — **primary-pending**, reconfirm before build). → columbiacommunityconnection.com (citing The Oregonian)
- After public-records requests (Sept 2021), the **city of The Dalles brought a reverse public-records lawsuit against the regional newspaper *The Oregonian* and its reporter** to keep the figures secret; **Google funded the city's legal costs**. The fight lasted **~13 months**. The **district attorney ruled the water use was not a trade secret**; the city settled/dropped it **14 Dec 2022**. Afterward Google told the Associated Press it would **stop treating site-level water use as a trade secret at its data centers nationwide**. → https://www.rcfp.org/dalles-google-oregonian-settlement/ · https://businessjournalism.org/2023/11/oregonian-data-centers/
- **[SOURCED / primary-pending]** The city's total legal fees are reported at **$153,000, paid by Google**; a separate account breaks this out as ~$106K defense + a $53K payment to the Reporters Committee for Freedom of the Press. Etch no single dollar figure until a primary settles it; ">$100k, paid by Google" is the safe floor.
- **CORRECTION (Verifier, binding).** The suit's defendant was **the newspaper and its reporter, not "the town's own residents."** That residents were the ones denied the information is **commentary**, not the identity of the party sued — keep the two strictly separate. (Reporter's personal name stays citation-only, per the named-individuals policy.)

**Constructed / IMAGINED form (carries sourced facts, asserts none):** the jittering-needle instrument; the case-file presentation; any request-form ritual + live counter. Marked as the art's form, not fact.

## The takedown law (self-written + refutation, as judged)
- **Takedown:** *"A FOIA-request LARP with a nice fountain attached — the audience already knows companies hide numbers, and performing bureaucratic delay for gallery-goers aestheticizes inconvenience at no cost to the artist."*
- **Refutation (Kritiker: partially, Act 1 carries it):** **(a) finding** — first object to put the field's ~70x self-disagreement on one calibrated, real-time instrument (most coverage quotes one number in isolation); **(b) risk upward** — names a company funding a municipality's legal effort to withhold a number, until a DA overruled it (real reputational risk in the *content*); **(c) form only this machinery produces** — a live needle refusing to settle in front of a body that just took one certain sip of real water; the LARP charge cannot touch the fountain (real water, no delay). **Act 2 as originally written (locked box, never opens) does NOT refute the takedown** — it free-rides on Act 1 and survives only because it is *true*, which was never the bar. Hence binding condition 3.
