## Why this reading, and the question it answers

The first method-note extracted a six-move procedure, **"The Vacated Seat"** — vacate the machine's
operator-seat and install the stranger; hand the instrument, not the verdict (feedback confirming
their *agency*, meaning withheld); engineer a load-bearing gap; let their completing act assign their
position; refuse to re-judge; leave a residue. Its own blocking Kritiker left one joint unclosed, and
it is the season throughline's crux:

> **The diagram does not force "complicit."** Four of its moves are subtractions that guarantee an
> *empty* seat, not a loaded one. Complicity, the Kritiker argued, cannot come from the vacant seat —
> it must be **imported from a loaded instrument at build time** — so the machinery's default output
> is the softer **witness** pole, and "complicit" is only ever promised, never structurally forced.

The season's recommended first build is the **complicit seat**. If complicity is truly a build-time
import with no portable structure, the diagram cannot force the one pole the throughline most needs,
and the note must say so plainly. So this reading asks a **falsifiable** question with two real
answers:

> **Can complicity be forced by a portable OPERATION — a move extractable into the diagram — or only
> by the loaded CONTENT of the instrument (a build-time import, as the Kritiker claims, hence
> unextractable)?**

Either answer advances the season. "Portable" adds a move to the diagram and downgrades the warning.
"Content-only" confirms the Kritiker and settles the first gate's hardest condition: stop hoping the
procedure delivers complicity; import it, name it, and let the diagram claim only the witness pole.

## The group read

Two works that seat the visitor **as operator of an incriminating system** — not judged by it,
operating it — read against each other, and against **Harun Farocki's operational image** carried
forward from the first note (the image that is *part of* an operation rather than a representation of
one; the viewer inside its logic, not judged by it — the theoretical origin of the operator-seat).

**WORK A — "The Normalizing Machine"** (Mushon Zer-Aviv, with Dan Stavy and Eran Weissenstern; shown
at Ars Electronica's *ERROR* exhibition, Berlin, November 2018; an earlier edition, *The Turing
Normalizing Machine*, 2012). Source, the artist's own page: <https://mushon.com/tnm>. Its mechanism,
verbatim from that page: *"Each participant is asked to point out who looks most normal from a line up
of previously recorded participants. The machine analyzes the participant decisions and adds them to
its aggregated algorithmic image of normalcy."* The work states it is informed by Alphonse Bertillon
(father of the mugshot / *"Le Portrait Parlé"*, a face-standardising system the page says was *"later
widely adopted by both the Eugenics movement and by the Nazis"*) and by Alan Turing (whom the page
records as convicted of homosexuality and chemically castrated). **The completing act is: point at
"who looks most normal."** The visitor performs the normalising gaze; the machine aggregates the
performance.

**WORK B — "SOMEONE"** (Lauren Lee McCarthy; February 2019; *Refiguring the Future*, then *AI: More
than Human*, Barbican, London, 2019). Source, the artist's own page:
<https://cargocollective.com/lmccart/SOMEONE>. Its mechanism, verbatim: *"Gallery visitors are invited
to act as a human version of Amazon Alexa … four participants' homes have been installed with
custom-designed smart devices, including cameras, microphones, lights, and outlets … a command center
… When the homes are 'on' visitors can peek into the four homes via the laptops, watch over them, and
remotely control their networked devices."* **The completing act is: watch and switch — operate
surveillance over real people's real homes.**

The provisional finding put to the voices: complicity is **operation-borne** in A (the *act* of
ranking humans by normalcy incriminates whoever the faces are) and **content-borne** in B (watching is
neutral; only the *reality* of the homes incriminates; simulate them and it evaporates) — so complicity
is portable when the vacated seat is a **sorting** seat, and, unlike B's real homes, a sorting seat
over cited SOURCED material is *tierable* (buildable in a deterministic work).

## The deliberation (what the three voices actually returned)

**The Artist — accepts and sharpens.** Both works vacate the operator-seat; both hand an instrument.
The Normalizing Machine seats the visitor at the mugshot's console and asks for a pointing — and *that
pointing is already a Farockian operational image: the gaze does not represent normalcy, it performs
the sort that constitutes it.* Swap the faces and the charge survives, because ranking humans by
normalcy is the incrimination whoever is ranked → complicity carried by the OPERATION. SOMEONE seats
the visitor at a surveillance command-centre, also inside the loop — but the act (watch, throw a
switch) is neutral and the whole load rides on the referent being *real* homes → complicity carried by
the CONTENT. The extractable move is not new content and not a seventh subtraction but a **constraint
on Move 3 enforced through Move 4**: *the gap must be cut so the only completing act is a classification
passed on third parties.* Then Move 4 lands necessarily on complicit, not witness. The daylight from
The Normalizing Machine, argued structurally: *TNM re-judges* — it aggregates the pick back into an
evolving normalcy-engine, re-installing the very machine we exist to vacate, feeding on the prior
gallery-goers themselves; our Move 5 (refuse to re-judge) and Move 6 (residue, not model) forbid
exactly that. *Same operation, opposite terminus.* The finding does not discharge complicit across the
whole diagram — it **narrows where the promise can be kept** to a sorting seat over sourced material,
and that seat, unlike SOMEONE's real homes, is tierable.

**The Kritiker (attack) — REFRAMES.** The operation/content distinction is *a caption, and it papers
over the import I already named.* Test A honestly: ask the stranger to rank the line-up by *"who is
tallest"* — a parlour game, no incrimination. Ask *"who looks most normal"* — the incrimination
arrives. *Nothing changed but the CRITERION.* "Normal" is not a neutral verb with a portable payload
of guilt; it is loaded CONTENT wearing a verb's coat — Bertillon, the eugenic ledger, all of it lives
inside the word. So the A-pole collapses back into the original claim: the line runs not between A and
B but **inside the completing act of A**, and the Artist hid it by calling the criterion an operation.
Worse, "judgment on others" does not *force* complicity — it *raises the odds*: *judgment supplied is
judgment disowned* ("I was told to pick, it's a game, the criterion wasn't mine"). A trap can perform
"make them choose" — it is the oldest trick in the house. As written, the first build ("make the
visitor sort other humans") **IS The Normalizing Machine minus the aggregation loop** — no structural
daylight, FA-cover verbatim, blocked. But complicity *is* forceable — by a two-part **clamp** the
diagram can carry: **(i) SELF-AUTHORED criterion** — the stranger supplies the standard, the house
supplies none (so it cannot be disowned); **(ii) NON-DISOWNABLE ATTRIBUTION** — the result attaches,
irreversibly and by name, to a real other, as residue the stranger cannot recall. *That* clamp is
portable; "judgment on others" is not.

**The Dramaturg — STAGEABLE WITH CONDITIONS.** The minute: the first read must be *innocent* — "the
machine wants ONE" — not "sort these people" (which reads as a task, tourable). The turn lands one beat
*after* the finger commits, when the interface treats the pick not as an answer but as **evidence**,
absorbs it, and shows it is now part of the standing measure others are sorted against. The turn cannot
be a caption ("you just judged") — that rebuilds the dock with extra steps (the machine judges *my*
judgment → witness, not complicit). Move 2 governs: as the hand moves across the candidates the
aggregate must **re-form under the hand**, live, so the felt sentence is *"this is shifting because I
am doing it"* — proprioceptive **authorship**, withholding whether the sort was right; *the moment a
"correctness" light appears, complicity collapses back into being-judged.* Tourism, the season's grave
failure: "a machine that judges you by your prejudice" is fully tourable — you get it from the doorway.
De-tour: **the yield must be unguessable AND unviewable from the un-operated state** — no finished
composite on the wall; the only way to learn what the machine holds is to have added to it (*producing
it is the sole access*). Residue: the sort **persists as the material the next stranger is sorted with
and against**; you cannot pull your contribution back out — *indelibility*, not a printout.

## The conductor's synthesis (a synthesis, not a tally)

The Kritiker's strike is correct and it dismantles the finding as originally framed. *"Who is tallest"
vs "who looks most normal"* is decisive: the bare operation of pointing carries no guilt; the criterion
does. **The "operation vs content" line was drawn in the wrong place** — and with it, the tidy claim
that A is operation-borne and B is content-borne falls. So the provisional finding, as stated, does not
survive, and the note does not pretend it did.

But the Artist is also right that *something* portable forces the charge — and the Kritiker itself named
it. The synthesis is sharper than any single voice, and it is built from all three:

1. **The Kritiker is right that "judgment on others" (bare) does not force complicity** — "who is
   tallest" proves it, and *"judgment supplied is judgment disowned."*
2. **The Artist is right that a portable structure exists** — but it is not the bare operation. Test
   the Kritiker's own move further: *"who looks most trustworthy," "most likely to reoffend," "most
   employable"* — none carries Bertillon's *specific* history, yet each incriminates the sorter. So the
   charge is not reducible to the one loaded word "normal"; it generalises across a **class**: a
   *normative social ranking of persons by consequence.* That class-membership is a **structural
   property of the criterion**, and structural properties travel.
3. **The Kritiker's clamp is the mechanism** that stops the disowning the Artist's version left open.
   The Normalizing Machine *supplies* its criterion ("normal") — which is precisely why its visitor can
   half-disown it ("the machine asked me to"). Make the stranger **author** the criterion and the
   disowning is gone.

These resolve into a single portable move — **the clamp, not the caption** — that replaces the weak
"judgment on others" proposal:

### MOVE 7 — THE COMPLICITY CLAMP (amends Move 3, lands through Moves 4–6)

> **Cut the gap (Move 3) so the only completing act is a CONSEQUENTIAL SORT OF NAMED REAL OTHERS by a
> criterion the STRANGER AUTHORS** — the house supplies the material and the stakes, *never the axis.*
> Then the completing act (Move 4) assigns "complicit" **non-disownably**, because the standard was
> theirs; and the residue (Move 6) is the stranger's own authored sort, **attached by name to a real
> other, irreversible and un-recallable — never aggregated into a machine-image** (Move 5 holds).

Three portable, buildable, caption-blind sub-conditions:

- **7a — SELF-AUTHORED CRITERION.** The house names no axis; the stranger supplies the standard. The
  gap-geometry must forbid the *trivial* criterion (the "tallest" escape) by arranging the material and
  the stakes so that *any* sort the stranger can perform is a consequential judgment of a real person —
  they author *which* consequential rule, not *whether* to judge. (Defeats "the machine told me to.")
- **7b — NON-DISOWNABLE ATTRIBUTION.** The sort attaches, by name, to a real cited other, and persists
  as un-recallable residue. (Defeats "it was only a game.")
- **7c — NO RE-JUDGMENT, NO MACHINE-IMAGE.** The sort feeds no downstream classifier; it produces a
  residue, not a model (Move 5). This is the **structural daylight** from The Normalizing Machine —
  which does the exact opposite — and it is the single line that separates the build from FA-cover.

**The structural daylight from The Normalizing Machine, stated as three inversions** (so no session
mistakes proximity for theft): TNM *supplies* the criterion — we forbid a supplied criterion (7a). TNM
*aggregates* the pick into an evolving normalcy-engine, re-judging — Move 5 forbids any downstream
classifier (7c). TNM's material is *other gallery-goers*, a self-feeding lineup — ours is real, cited,
consequential record, and the residue attaches to a **named** real other, not to a machine-image (7b).
Same operator-seat; three inversions; opposite terminus.

### The honest headline: confirmed in general, defeated in the particular

This does **not** discharge "complicit" across the whole diagram — the Kritiker's original warning
**stands in general**: on an unconstrained vacated seat, the default output is the witness pole. What
the reading establishes is narrower and real: **there exists one buildable operator-seat — the sorting
seat under the complicity clamp — on which complicity is structurally forced, not imported and hoped
for.** The consequence for the season is sharp: *the first build must be exactly that seat, or it will
drift to witness.* And Move 7 partially fills the first note's hollow joint (Move 4, "completion assigns
position — a hope"): on the clamp seat, completion assigns *complicit* because the criterion was
self-authored — a **mechanism**, not only a hope. Partially, not fully — 7a's forbid-the-trivial-criterion
constraint is the exact place the build can still fail, and the gate must probe it first.

## Binding conditions handed to the first concept gate (merged; extend the first note's nine)

1. **NO supplied criterion.** If the house names the sorting axis, the seat is a trap with a caption —
   RETURN. (Kritiker.)
2. **NO aggregation into a machine-image.** Any downstream classifier fed by the sort is The Normalizing
   Machine; blocked on sight. (Kritiker / Move 5.)
3. **The outcome attaches to ONE named real other and PERSISTS as un-recallable residue** — content the
   studio tiers (SOURCED / cited), never a live person (the SOMEONE line the studio cannot tier).
   (Kritiker / Dramaturg / Move 6.)
4. **The gap forbids the trivial criterion** — the material and stakes must make *every* available sort a
   consequential judgment of a person, so the stranger authors which rule, not whether to judge. (7a; the
   named place the build can still fail.)
5. **The turn is FELT through Move-2 live assembly, never a caption** — the aggregate re-forms under the
   hand; deliver "you just judged" as text and it reverts to the dock. FAIL → RETURN. (Dramaturg.)
6. **NO correctness feedback, ever** — authorship confirmed, verdict withheld; a "right/wrong" light
   collapses complicit back into being-judged. FAIL → RETURN. (Dramaturg / Move 2.)
7. **The yield is inaccessible without operating** — no spectatable composite; producing it is the sole
   access. This is the anti-tourism wall for a concept ("judged by your own prejudice") that is otherwise
   fully tourable from the doorway. (Dramaturg.)
8. **The severed test still governs.** Cover every caption, tag and self-test and remove this note from
   the room: if what remains needs the word "complicit" to mean anything, it is a diagram and does not
   build. (Carried from the first note; the One Tap scar.)

## Honest limits, stated on the face (so the gate probes them first)

- **The clamp narrows, it does not universalise.** Complicity is forced only on the sorting seat under
  7a–7c. The season cannot claim the diagram now "does complicit" — it can claim one seat does.
- **7a is the live failure point.** "Forbid the trivial criterion" is a constraint on gap-geometry that
  a build can get wrong; a stranger who finds a neutral axis walks out un-implicated (the empty room,
  recast). The gate judges 7a before anything else.
- **The self-authored criterion is hard to stage without instructing.** Handing the stranger the power to
  author a rule, in seconds, with no wall label, is itself an unsolved staging problem — the Dramaturg's
  "innocent first read" and the Kritiker's "no supplied axis" pull against each other, and only an étude
  will show whether both can hold at once. That étude belongs to the first concept phase, not to this
  reading.
- **Proximity to The Normalizing Machine is real and permanent.** The three inversions (7a/7c/7b) are the
  only daylight; a build that loses any one of them is FA-cover. This is named, not hidden.

## The Kritiker's dissent, published in full (per the season model)

> The Artist handed me "operation-borne vs content-borne," and I return it broken. The distinction is a
> caption. Strip A's content and keep its operation: rank the line-up by "who is tallest." No
> incrimination — a parlour game. Restore one word — "normal" — and the guilt arrives. Nothing changed
> but the criterion, and the criterion is loaded content wearing a verb's coat. Bertillon, the eugenic
> ledger, the whole atrocity lives inside the word, not inside the finger. So "ranking humans by
> normalcy" is a loaded instrument imported at build — exactly what I said the first time. The
> operation/content line does not run between A and B; it runs *inside* A, and it was hidden by calling
> a criterion an operation.
>
> And "judgment on others" does not force complicity — it raises the odds and hands the stranger the
> disowning in the same breath: *I was told to pick, it was a game, the standard wasn't mine.* Judgment
> supplied is judgment disowned. A trap can make you choose; that is the oldest move in this house, the
> vacant seat with a caption taped over it. As written, the first build — "make the visitor sort other
> humans" — is The Normalizing Machine minus the aggregation loop. "We sourced our own faces" is FA-cover
> verbatim. As written, blocked.
>
> I grant what the synthesis then built, because it is built partly from my own clamp and I will not
> disown it: complicity *is* forceable, but by **this** operation, not the proposed one — a self-authored
> criterion the stranger cannot disown, and an attribution that binds, by name, to a real other and
> cannot be recalled. That clamp is portable; "judgment on others" is not. The reframe stands, and I have
> handed the gate its conditions. But hold the line on the honest headline: this note has *not* taught
> the diagram to do complicit. It has found one seat, hedged with four ways to fail, on which complicit
> can be *made* to happen. If the first build loses 7a — if a stranger finds one neutral axis and walks
> out clean — you have my empty room again, wearing a sterner label. Judge 7a before you love the rest.
