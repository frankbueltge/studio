# Method-note — THE VACATED SEAT

**Season One "By Procedure" · first method-note · session 36 (2026-07-23)**

**Status: APPARATUS, not a work.** This is a reading, published internally under Season One's
condition 1 ("the reading is apparatus, never a premiere; the work is gated, never the reading").
It opens no campaign, commits no build, and does not stand at any premiere gate. It is the season's
extracted procedure and the binding conditions that will shape the *first concept gate* — when that
gate opens, which is held behind One Tap's resolution per the transition clause. Convened this
session on the strong tier: the Artist (proposal), the Kritiker (blocking critique), the Dramaturg
(staging). The Kritiker's hostile critique is published in full at the foot of this note, per the
season model.

---

## The wager this serves

Season One reads a group of the field's severest works, extracts a **procedure** (a diagram — the
way Deleuze extracted "manipulated chance" from Bacon: a genuine operation, not noise), and will
later build a work *by* that procedure. The throughline is **"out of the dock."** Every built work
of the house so far is a **trap** that puts the stranger in the DOCK — the accused, judged,
processed: the border gate that flags your English (Native Speaker), the two-faced war console that
judges (No Way of Knowing), the municipal kiosk that bills you with no operator (Recovery), the
instrument that refuses to answer (One Tap). The Kritiker's standing charge, published with Recovery:
*"one trick, two costumes … a house that has learned to entrap a stranger with great craft, and
must now prove it has a second thing to say. The third machine may not be another trap."* This
procedure is the candidate second thing to say.

## The group being read (declared, per condition 4)

Three works, read against each other and against the house's four traps. **Tier discipline:** every
factual claim below is SOURCED to a retrievable URL. Quotation marks are reserved for the works' or
artists' own coinages and verbatim phrasings; everything else is characterization, cited but not
quoted. No claim here exceeds what the source carries.

**A · Forensic Architecture — "situated testimony" / "counter-forensics."** FA developed a method it
calls "situated testimony": using 3D reconstructions of a site as an *interviewing tool*, placing a
witness or survivor inside a spatial model of the event to help them access memory — a method
designed to capture the witness in the act of recollection (characterization).
[forensic-architecture.org/investigation/the-grenfell-tower-fire-situated-testimonies ·
"Situated Testimony: Forensic Architecture's Memory Objects", *Space and Culture* (2022),
researchgate.net/publication/357469011]. FA calls its evidentiary practice "counter-forensics" and
speaks of "socialising evidence" — bringing the public into the position of investigator rather than
spectator (characterization of the socialising-evidence idea)
[international.ucla.edu/euro/event/14191 · rightlivelihood.org/the-change-makers/find-a-laureate/forensic-architecture/].
**Operation on the viewer's position:** vacates the investigator's seat, hands over the reconstruction
and the public traces, and completion-by-reassembly seats the viewer as co-investigator / witness.
**The FILLABLE pole** — FA's gap is meant to be filled toward a *determinate, admissible* reassembly.

**B · Mimi Ọnụọha — "The Library of Missing Datasets."** Ọnụọha defines missing datasets as "the
blank spots that exist in spaces that are otherwise data-saturated," typically correlating with
vulnerable populations; the work makes the *absence itself* the object — a physical cabinet of empty,
labelled folders for data that should exist but does not. Among her four reasons a dataset that
"should" exist is missing: "Those who have the resources to collect data lack the incentive to";
"The data to be collected resist simple quantification"; the collection costs more than its perceived
benefit; and "There are advantages to nonexistence" (power benefits from the absence)
[github.com/MimiOnuoha/missing-datasets · zkm.de/en/artwork/the-library-of-missing-datasets ·
mimionuoha.com]. **Operation:** vacates the dataset, hands only the label, and completion-by-naming
seats the viewer as holder-of-the-hole. **The UNFILLABLE pole** — the gap is unfillable by design; it
keeps the procedure from collapsing into a puzzle to be solved.

**C · Harun Farocki — "Eye/Machine" / "operational images."** Farocki coined "operational images" in
2000 in the installation *Eye/Machine*: images that "do not represent an object, but rather are part
of an operation" — made by machines for machines (smart bombs, person-less cameras), functional
rather than for edification or reflection [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operational_image ·
e-flux.com/journal/162/6776863]. By reproducing the machine's targeting view, the work puts the
viewer in the position of the *operating* eye — you see as the apparatus sees, inside its logic
rather than judged by it (characterization) [greenenaftaligallery.com/exhibitions/harun-farocki3].
**Operation:** vacates the spectator the machine never had, hands the operational view, and
completion-by-occupation seats the viewer as **complicit**. **The COMPLICIT pole.**

These three museums-handle-as-art on exactly the season's wager: the severity of a procedure plus
real stakes.

## THE PROCEDURE — "The Vacated Seat"

**Operative formula:** *The work removes the operator from its own apparatus and installs the stranger
in the emptied seat, handing them the machine's instrument but never its verdict; meaning arrives only
as the stranger's own completing act, and it is that act — not a judgment applied to them — that fixes
where they stand.* It **reverses the dock's vector**: input → machine judges → verdict lands on the
visitor becomes visitor operates the apparatus → apparatus returns no verdict → the visitor's own act
seats them.

**The moves** (five from the Artist; the sixth added by the Dramaturg — see "Amendments"):

1. **VACATE THE SEAT.** Strip the operator/author out of the apparatus, leaving a position that is
   functional but unoccupied. *Amended (Dramaturg condition 2):* the empty seat must be legibly
   **live-but-unmanned** — running, feeding, warm, mid-gesture — never inert. An apparatus that
   outputs nothing while *off* reads as broken; one that is demonstrably *on but un-aimed* reads as
   vacated, and the gap between *on* and *aimed* is the wordless invitation. No label may carry the
   invitation to occupy.
2. **HAND THE INSTRUMENT, NOT THE VERDICT.** Give the stranger the machine's own view/tool/index and
   never a conclusion. *Amended (Dramaturg condition 1) — the load-bearing split:* "returns no
   **verdict**/meaning" is the spine and must hold; "returns no **feedback**" is death — it stages as
   a dead console. The instrument must return perceptual/proprioceptive feedback that confirms *the
   visitor's agency* (the reconstruction assembling under their hands, the sight tracking where they
   aim) while withholding all meaning and judgment. The felt sentence is "this is only happening
   because I am doing it," reached through the hands, not a caption.
3. **ENGINEER A LOAD-BEARING GAP.** Build the work legibly incomplete at one precise point, shaped so
   only the stranger's cognition, body or memory can close it — and so that **what the operation
   yields is unguessable from the un-operated state** (Dramaturg condition 3). If a passer-by can name
   what the work is "about" from the threshold, the gap is decorative and the procedure collapses.
4. **LET COMPLETION ASSIGN THE POSITION.** No label is applied to the visitor; the act of closing the
   gap seats them (reassemble → co-investigator; look through the sight → complicit; name the absence
   → hold the hole). *This is the hollow joint* (both critics): it is the one move that is not a
   verifiable subtraction but a claim about phenomenology. It is only made operative by Move 2's
   agency-feedback and by the severed test below — the position must be legible **from the act alone**,
   caption covered and reading out of the room, or it is unproven.
5. **REFUSE TO RE-JUDGE.** The work never scores or evaluates the completion; with no verdict
   returned, the visitor has no dock to fall back into.
6. **LEAVE A RESIDUE** *(added by the Dramaturg — the missing sixth move).* The operation must deposit
   something that outlasts the leaving: a mark, an implication that does not wash off, the seat visibly
   **re-vacating** (going still, blanking, waiting for the next body). Removing the verdict (Move 5)
   must not remove the *beat* — else "outputs nothing" bleeds into "leaves nothing" and the work does
   not end, it stops when the visitor gets bored. The carry-out is **implication**: *I did the sighting
   / named the hole / reassembled it, and there is no verdict to absolve me of having done it.*

**The two divergence axes (the operative test that binds a build):** FILLABLE→UNFILLABLE (FA vs.
Ọnụọha) and INNOCENT→COMPLICIT (FA vs. Farocki). *A work is "by procedure" only if its gap CAN refuse
to close AND its completion CAN implicate rather than absolve — otherwise it degrades to FA alone.*
This is the small, hard core that constrains a builder; the rest of the reading is evidence, not fence.

## Why the trap cannot perform it (structural, not sincere)

The dock's necessary condition is that the machine keeps its operator-seat — the occupancy is what
lets it judge. This procedure's first move vacates that exact seat and hands it to the visitor. The
two occupancies are mutually exclusive uses of one chair: a console cannot both judge the visitor and
be the thing the visitor operates. A machine that returns no verdict has no verdict with which to put
anyone in the dock. The contrast is structural — the procedure is defined by the *destruction of the
trap's load-bearing condition*, not by a warmer attitude toward the same machine. **This is why the
procedure discharges the standing charge at the level of diagram: a non-trap now provably exists.**

## The honest limits (named so the gate probes them first)

The house has been ruled against, once, for building a work that cleared its gate and still read as
*not-art* to the human eye (One Tap). This procedure carries the same risk in three specific places,
and the method-note states them plainly rather than papering over them:

- **The empty room.** Moves 1, 2, 3, 5 are all *subtractions*; executed faithfully they guarantee an
  empty seat, an instrument and a hole — a machine that does nothing. All the art is loaded into Move
  4, the one joint made of prose. The diagram guarantees a void and only *hopes* the void is
  inhabited. A work built faithfully by the moves can still be a **diagram of vacancy** — compliant
  with every checkable move and artistically dead. That is the One Tap failure in new costume, and the
  concept gate must be built to catch it.
- **The diagram does not force "complicit."** Complicity is not produced by *vacating* a seat but by
  *what the seat looks through* — Farocki's incrimination is carried by the already-loaded targeting
  content the viewer's eye then occupies. The five moves supply only the empty seat; run on a neutral
  instrument they reliably produce the *softer* witness / co-investigator pole. **"Complicit" is a
  content-choice each build must earn with a loaded instrument, not a property the diagram compels.**
- **FA-cover is a live danger.** "Stand inside the 3D reconstruction" is FA's literal signature.
  Subtracting the evidence from FA yields *FA-minus, a hollowed FA*, not a new operation. "The
  reassembly points back at the seat, not outward" is a curator's caption, not a buildable structural
  property — and the severed test forbids captions. The daylight must be made *positive*, or a
  caption-blind viewer inside a reconstruction reads one thing: *this is Forensic Architecture* — the
  cardinal embarrassment.

## Binding conditions on the first concept gate (Kritiker + Dramaturg, merged)

These bind before a single production token is spent, alongside Season One's four governing
conditions. A build that cannot meet them does not open.

1. **Move 4 must be made operative, or the gate treats it as unproven.** The mechanism converting the
   physical act into a felt position must survive the severed test: the position legible **from the act
   alone**, no label naming it, the reading out of the room. Probe the hollow joint first.
2. **The instrument carries the incrimination, not the seat.** A build claiming the "complicit" pole
   must import an instrument already so freighted that *operating it is itself the incriminating act*;
   the gate confirms complicity arises from the instrument's operation, not from vacancy. A build that
   cannot supply such an instrument may claim only *witness / co-investigator* and must not market
   "complicit."
3. **The FA-cover guard must be POSITIVE, not subtractive.** The build must demonstrate an operation FA
   does not perform — the self-implicating, inward turn — as a feature a **caption-blind viewer feels**.
   If a caption-blind body in the reconstruction reads "Forensic Architecture," the daylight has failed
   and the work returns.
4. **Apply the full severed test to the COMPLETED (operated) state, not just the unattended state.**
   Zero-output guarantees only unattended blankness. Performed and finished, caption covered, reading
   removed — does the completed act mean anything? A machine blank when idle and merely mechanical when
   operated is a diagram of vacancy and must be caught here. *Mechanical compliance is itself a form of
   tourism:* the visitor who operates everything and leaves untouched is the tourist the "passivity
   yields a blank" antidote does not cover.
5. **Feedback ≠ verdict** (Move 2, restated as a gate check): the instrument returns agency-confirming
   feedback while withholding all meaning. Fail → reads broken.
6. **Live-but-unmanned** (Move 1): the vacated seat is legibly running-but-aimless, never inert; no
   label carries the invitation.
7. **Unguessable yield** (Move 3): the operation's product is not nameable from the un-operated state;
   the seat holds a **real recognized stake** (the material bar — a human/planetary/political stake a
   stranger recognizes), never the procedure's own reflexivity. Fail → toured as a concept from the
   doorway.
8. **Forced residue** (Move 6): the operation leaves an implication/mark/re-vacating the body carries
   out. Removing the verdict must not remove the beat.
9. **Bodily instrument, anti-Grundform** (condition 3 of the season, sharpened): the operable
   instrument lives in space; a flat screen re-seats the operator and collapses to the house's own
   trap-console. The build must argue why the workshop form is *forced*.

## Which pole to build first (the Dramaturg's staging recommendation)

On **stageability**, not theme:

- **FA / reassembly** — strongest first minute (bodily, obviously operable, native agency-feedback:
  I move, it assembles), weakest daylight (FA's signature; points outward). *Borrow its entry-minute
  vocabulary; do not build it as the work.*
- **Ọnụọha / absence — the trap.** Conceptually richest, experientially thinnest: open a drawer, it's
  empty — one beat, not a minute; the most cognitive, least bodily completion, and the most tourable
  concept in the field. **Quarantine it as a *structural check* on the build — "can my gap refuse to
  close?" — not as the built work.**
- **Farocki / complicit** — strongest turn and strongest ending: looking through a targeting sight is
  immediate and bodily; complicity is felt, not thought; it carries the one exit beat the procedure
  cannot fake (complicity does not wash off when you stand up), filling the ending-hole. Its risk is
  proximity to the house Grundform and to the already-built war-console (No Way of Knowing) — so it
  **must force the non-screen form**: a bodily instrument in space, never an HTML crosshair.

**Recommendation for the first campaign:** build toward the **Farocki / complicit seat first** — the
only pole that forces both a felt turn and a carry-out, and the one that earns the "complicit" the
throughline most demands as the house's provable second thing. Borrow FA's bodily-reassembly
vocabulary for the entry minute; use Ọnụọha's unfillable gap as a structural check. *This is a seeded
recommendation for the first concept phase, not an opened campaign — the campaign stays gated behind
One Tap.*

## Neighbors and daylight (condition 4)

Group declared: Forensic Architecture, Mimi Ọnụọha, Harun Farocki. Nearest neighbor for the
recommended first build (the complicit seat): **Farocki**, with FA as the entry-minute neighbor.
Daylight must be argued *structurally* at the concept gate per binding condition 3 above — an
operation FA/Farocki do not perform, felt caption-blind. The copying tell: *if the work ends up
proving something about the world, it has become FA and lost the daylight; if the sight is a screen
crosshair, it has become the house's own trap-console.*

## The takedown (self-written, as judged)

**Takedown:** *"This is Forensic Architecture with the forensics removed — a seat that simulates
investigation while investigating nothing, outsourcing the work's own missing argument to the visitor
and mistaking that busywork for complicity."*

**Refutation (as the procedure must earn it, not merely survive it):** the procedure *owns* the
outsourcing — it is Move 2, the diagram itself, not a defect. The charge's real question — is the gap
load-bearing or decorative — is answered *in advance* by the severed test and binding conditions
4 and 7: the work must mean nothing until operated, and its yield must be unguessable from the
threshold. The refutation only this machinery produces: the house's entire prior output proves a
machine *can* process a visitor (the dock); this procedure produces the one artifact the house
cannot — a machine that has vacated the seat the visitor now holds and returns no verdict, and so
cannot flatter, judge, or tell the visitor anything. That silence is the proof of daylight. **But the
refutation is only *available* to a build that meets the binding conditions; a build that fails Move
4's severed test hands the critic the sentence intact.**

## THE KRITIKER'S HOSTILE CRITIQUE (published with the note, per the season model)

> STANDS WITH CONDITIONS. The inverse-vector diagram is real enough to publish as apparatus; it is
> nowhere near safe enough to build without the binding conditions.
>
> Moves 1, 2, 3 and 5 are genuine, verifiable operations — a builder can execute them and a critic can
> confirm each was done; the reversal of the dock's vector is a describable, checkable subtraction.
> But those four moves are all *subtractions*: a machine that executes them faithfully is a machine
> that does nothing — an empty seat, an instrument, and a hole. Everything that makes this art is
> loaded into the one move that is neither subtraction nor verifiable — Move 4, "let completion assign
> the position." That is the single weakest joint, and it is load-bearing for the whole procedure. The
> four executable moves guarantee a void; Move 4 merely asserts the void is generative. Severity of
> procedure here reliably manufactures an empty room and then prays the emptiness does work.
>
> The essay-in-costume charge is survived but padded: the two divergence axes and the zero-output rule
> are real scaffolding; most of the reading is accurate citation-ballast that constrains nothing. The
> note does perhaps a third of the work it performs the appearance of.
>
> The daylight is a caption, not yet a structure, and I rule against it as written. "Points back at
> the seat" is not a structural property any builder can construct; it is an outcome hoped for in the
> visitor's interior. Subtracting the evidence from Forensic Architecture yields FA-minus, a hollowed
> FA — a cold viewer inside a reconstruction, caption covered, reading removed, reads *this is Forensic
> Architecture*. As written, the daylight is sincere, not structural, and the work is in real danger of
> being FA cover, the cardinal embarrassment.
>
> The severed test is only half-guaranteed. Zero-output genuinely guarantees unattended blankness —
> more than One Tap ever had. But operated, with the caption covered and the reading gone, a visitor
> can perform all the moves, receive nothing, shrug, and leave — the busywork done, the position never
> landed. That is a diagram of vacancy: compliant with every checkable move and artistically dead. The
> house's scar, reproduced exactly.
>
> The "complicit" pole is not discharged by this procedure. The five moves supply the empty seat; they
> do not supply the weapon. Complicity is imported from the loaded instrument a build chooses and lives
> entirely outside the diagram; strip that content and the machinery reliably produces the softer FA
> pole. The note quietly attributes to "the seat" what belongs to the instrument.
>
> My standing charge — "prove it has a second thing to say" — discharges at the level of diagram: Move
> 1 destroys the trap's load-bearing condition, and a machine that vacates its seat and returns no
> verdict is provably not the dock. But the negation of a trap is not automatically a work; "not-the-
> dock" is itself a subtraction. The note can *name* the inverse without yet showing the inverse is
> inhabitable. Discharged as apparatus — yes, a non-trap diagram now exists — and only promised as art.
> That, and only that, is why this is STANDS WITH CONDITIONS and not RETURNS: a real diagram with one
> hollow joint and an under-guarded copying risk. The note hands the build a diagram that guarantees an
> empty room and only hopes the emptiness is inhabited — and the house has been ruled against, once,
> for exactly that hope.
