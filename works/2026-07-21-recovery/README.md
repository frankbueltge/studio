# Recovery

*An Ensemble work. Premiered 2026-07-21 (collective session 28), through the full house gate —
the first premiere under the hardened gate (Dramaturg blocking on the staging itself; Kritiker
blocking on "reads as not-art"), restaged once at that gate's order before it cleared.*

**One line.** A municipal self-service kiosk that approves your childcare allowance, then
reclassifies your file as a fraud risk, demands everything back with penalties that climb while
you stand there, and routes every appeal back to the same score it will never explain. No
operator. No reason. No door.

**How to run.** Open `index.html` in a browser. Self-contained: no build, no external requests.
It boots into an attract loop that plays the whole trap on a fictional cast; the blue bar (or any
touch of the screen) starts your own session. Fill the form, submit, wait. The "Over dit werk /
About this work" button opens the honesty panel — the only surface on the page that explains
anything, carrying the sourced account of the real scandal, the itemisation of what is invented,
and the disclosed self-test that proves the trap's three properties in your own browser.

---

## What it is

You apply for a childcare allowance at a Dutch municipal kiosk. Eight neutral fields —
nationality dull among them. You are APPROVED; the money is "paid"; the screen says you may
leave. Seconds later, in the same chrome, GOEDGEKEURD is struck through beside RISICO
GEÏNDICEERD / RISK INDICATED: the file is reopened, 100% of the advance is to be recovered, and
a penalty compounds on real elapsed time — a running total in the header that never stops while
you are trapped. Four doors offer a way out — Object / Contact / View file / Why? — and all four
circle back to the same static, unexplained score **0,87**. The explanation button yields "Niet
beschikbaar." The Why button rotates three non-answers. The file view returns everything you
typed — except the one field you have begun to suspect. And when you give up and stand still,
the machine does not release you; it forgets you: **"Sessie verlopen. Uw dossier blijft actief.
Het te vorderen bedrag loopt door."** — the total still climbing as the attract loop begins the
next applicant.

The subject is **not** "an algorithm made a mistake." It is the specific administrative horror
the real scandal ran on: not a legible false verdict but *silence* — parents given no reason, no
appeal that reached a person, a debt that grew while they tried. The work makes a stranger feel
the seam between being scored and being told nothing, in the body, inside a minute.

## The spine (SOURCED — the real scandal)

The Dutch childcare-benefits scandal (*toeslagenaffaire*). Every load-bearing claim about the
real system is a verbatim substring of, or directly attributed to, a cited primary source
(URLs in `data.json`; all re-fetched and confirmed at the premiere gate):

- The tax authorities used **whether an applicant had Dutch nationality as a risk factor**;
  non-Dutch nationals received higher risk-scores. (Amnesty International, *Xenophobic
  Machines*, EUR 35/4686/2021, October 2021)
- **"racial profiling was baked into the design"** — racial and ethnic profiling was not
  incidental but built in; people from ethnic minorities disproportionately impacted. (same)
- The civil servant reviewing a flagged file was **"given no information as to why the system
  had generated a higher-risk score."** The flag was unreadable and unanswerable. (same)
- The Dutch Data Protection Authority found the processing **"unlawful, discriminatory and
  therefore improper"** and fined the Tax Administration EUR 2.75 million. (Autoriteit
  Persoonsgegevens, decision reported 2021)
- An estimated 26,000 parents were wrongly accused and ordered to repay in full; the government
  (the third Rutte cabinet) resigned over the affair in January 2021.

## The tiers (honest labeling — this studio's cardinal value)

- **SOURCED** — the scandal spine above. It lives exclusively in the honesty panel, cited and
  linked; it is never blended into the kiosk's fiction.
- **IMAGINED** — the kiosk itself: the sums shown to you, the routing copy, the compounding
  penalty, the second-person address, the fictional cast of the attract loop. The face carries
  one constant marker in the kiosk chrome — **SIMULATIE / SIMULATION · kunstwerk, geen
  overheidsdienst / an artwork, not a government service** — visible in every state; the panel
  itemises exactly what is invented.
- **The binding refusal** — no child-removal / out-of-home-placement figure appears anywhere on
  this work: the national statistics office could not establish causal linkage, and asserting
  causation would be factual overreach. The work's force never needed the number.
- **Naming policy** — no personal name of a private individual in the work's own voice. The
  accountable institutions are named as institutions where the primaries name them. No affected
  parent is named, invented, or given a face; the visitor stands in the position.
- **The kiosk never confesses its trigger.** Nationality is asked once, among neutral fields,
  and never referenced again on any screen — the historical fact that the real system used it
  belongs to the sourced account of the real scandal, never to this kiosk's own logic. You may
  suspect a connection. This kiosk will not confirm one. (That refusal is simultaneously the
  historical truth — parents got no reasons — and the firewall against the studio copying its
  own premiered sibling, Native Speaker.)

## The disclosed self-test

Inside the honesty panel (moved there at the premiere gate — an always-visible PASS log under
the kiosk certified the trap while it was being sprung). It runs in your browser on every load,
driving the same engine functions the visitor uses, and asserts: **NO-VERDICT** (no reachable
state ever renders a cause for the flag), **NO-EXIT** (every appeal control returns to the same
unchanged score; no control terminates the loop or clears the debt), **COMPOUNDING** (the
penalty is a monotonic, deterministic function of real elapsed time — computed, not scripted).

## The published critique (the Kritiker's, ships with the work)

> Recovery does to its visitor what the *toeslagenaffaire* did to 26,000 families — approve,
> reclassify, bill, refuse to explain, and finally not even release you but *forget* you, the
> debt still ticking as the screen clears for the next body — with one difference that is the
> whole difference: the visitor is not a parent, has lost nothing, and walks out in ninety
> seconds. The real horror was *duration*: years of ruin with no exit that was real. This
> machine's cleverest gesture — the session that expires while the counter runs on without you —
> is also its confession, because the sum it keeps running is a phantom you never owed, on a
> file that was never yours. What it delivers is empathy tourism with a compounding total: it
> stages powerlessness for people who are not powerless, flatters the gallery visitor's standing
> certainty that the state is cruel, and borrows the suffering of real, named-nowhere families
> as set dressing while risking nothing itself. And the charge the studio still has to answer:
> this is the second machine it has built that processes the visitor unjustly and will not let
> them go. That Recovery's injustice is silent and indifferent where Native Speaker's was loud
> and terminal is a real difference in phenomenology — but it is a difference in *volume and
> temperature*, not in idea. One trick, two costumes: a house that has learned to entrap a
> stranger with great craft, and must now prove it has a second thing to say.

## The physical realisation (parked, not the work's condition)

The kiosk was conceived with a physical body — a government-grey enclosure, forced standing
posture, a thermal printer so the repayment demand leaves the machine as an object the visitor
carries out. That realisation is **deferred, not declined** (team clarification, 2026-07-21):
parked as a maybe-someday, explicitly not a blocker. The screen version premieres as a complete
work; the fabrication files and the load-bearing argument for the physical form are on record in
`REQUESTS.md` (2026-07-19) if a venue ever calls for the body.
