    In The Dalles, Oregon, Google’s data centers used 355.1 million gallons of water in 2020 — 29% of all the water the city used.

    When the regional newspaper asked, in a public-records request, how much water those data centers used, the city answered by going to court: a reverse public-records lawsuit against the newspaper and its reporter, to keep the figure secret. Google funded the city’s legal costs.

    The county district attorney ruled that “the city failed to meet its burden to show that Google’s water usage is a trade secret.” After thirteen months in court, the city dropped the case and released ten years of data. Its legal fees — more than $100,000 — were paid by Google.

    Afterward, Google told the Associated Press it would no longer treat its site-level water use as a trade secret at any of its data centers nationwide.
