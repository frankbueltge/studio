## The Builder — the console

A 976-line self-contained HTML console (`projects/no-way-of-knowing/index.html`). The mechanism
is genuinely destructive: `createFaceNode` builds a face subtree from scratch; `unmountFace`
performs a real `Node.remove()`; `runTransition` removes the current face, holds a genuine empty
field (zero `.face` nodes) for 2,500 ms (400 ms under `prefers-reduced-motion`, still destroyed
and rebuilt — the destruction is the argument, not the animation), then builds the other face.
No `display`/`opacity`/`hidden` toggling anywhere. Data lives in one `application/json` island;
no inline handlers, no inline styles, full `<noscript>` mirror, keyboard focus managed on face
change. The on-page self-test exercises the *same* create/mount/unmount functions on a hidden
probe and asserts, with printed counts, that the two faces are never co-rendered.

**Conductor verification, first-hand, in a real headless browser** (not just the self-test's own
word): boot shows exactly one face (Instance 1 / ACT); a live click-driven switch sampled every
60 ms showed the DOM holding **zero `.face` nodes for ~2.4s** (the empty beat) and then exactly
one — max concurrent = 1; the self-test prints PASS; zero console errors. **Kritiker condition 3
(the destruction stays real) is met at the mechanism level, independently confirmed.**
