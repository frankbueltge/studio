## Why "One Tap", and the recast

The session-18 hold named the problem precisely: (1) the sip-vs-gulp rivalry ran on a *dramatized
ratio* — an invented core cannot be a work's spine (the standing decision from that same night);
(2) the claimed daylight was *concealed commons-capture*, and the plumbing form could not carry
it — make the secrecy felt or the work is "AI uses water", already known.

The conductor gathered the SOURCED material first-hand before any sub-agent tokens were spent,
and it reframed the whole concept. There **is** a real per-query water figure — several of them —
and they disagree by roughly **70x**: the peer-reviewed GPT-3 estimate is 7.1–47.5 mL per request
(Li et al., arXiv 2304.03271, who call their own figure "conservative"); a 2024 Washington
Post / UC Riverside figure is ~519 mL for a single 100-word GPT-4 email; and the estimate has
been *reported* revised sharply downward since (a ~15 mL figure, found only in secondary
reporting — carried as a *reported* revision, not a researcher's verbatim quote). Skeptics call
the high figure a miscalculation. That 70x spread exists **because companies do not disclose
per-query water**. And at facility scale, the concealment is documented and litigated: Google's
The Dalles, Oregon data centers drew 355.1M gallons in 2020 — 29% of the city's entire water
supply — and the city (its legal costs funded by Google) spent ~13 months in court trying to
keep even that annual number secret, until a district attorney ruled it was not a trade secret
(settled 14 Dec 2022; Google then said it would stop treating site-level water use as a trade
secret nationwide).

So the felt core moves from **"the machine drank more than me"** (false-precise, a tier violation
dressed as generosity) to **"I cannot find out what my question cost — and neither could a whole
town, in court, for thirteen months."** The contested number is not a defect to hide; it *is* the
secrecy, made legible on one instrument. Homework 1 and 2 answered each other.

## The deliberation

Three role sub-agents were convened on an efficient tier; the conductor ran the source-gathering
and both fact corrections first-hand.

**Artist — RECAST.** Keep the real fountain, the body, the real water; move the load-bearing
sensation to the unresolvability. Act 1: a jittering analog needle on a dial engraved with the
actual published range that *never settles*, because no disclosed value exists for it to rest on.
Act 2: the Dalles concealment case, told straight. Arc: you drink (certain, known) → you ask (no
answer, honestly) → you try to formally find out (structurally blocked, as a real newsroom was).
The Artist named its nearest neighbours unprompted (Crawford/Joler, Onuoha, Julian Oliver's
*Harvest* — the sharpest, a formal inversion: a physical process driven by the *absence* of a
true number rather than its presence — Paglen/Burrington, Disnovation) and argued daylight from
each.

**Verifier — BLOCKING → correctable.** Re-fetched the arXiv paper directly and confirmed the
per-request range verbatim (7.107 mL Ireland / 47.506 mL Washington), the "conservative /
several times higher" quote, the 355.1M gal = 29% (correctly attached to 2020), the 104.3M gal
2010 baseline, the 13-month fight, the DA ruling, the 14 Dec 2022 settlement. No single per-query
number was found presented as settled fact — the contested-range framing holds; the IMAGINED
form (needle, cabinet, ritual) is clearly marked as construction. **One BLOCKING finding, and it
was the right catch:** the brief's phrase "against the town's own residents" **mischaracterizes
the lawsuit** — the Google-funded reverse-records suit was brought against the regional newspaper
*The Oregonian* and its reporter, not against residents as a party. That residents were the ones
denied the information is *commentary*, not the identity of the defendant, and the two must stay
strictly separate. LOW findings: the dial mixes three different models/years/teams (label each
pin's model + year or the spread reads as measurement noise); the "$153,000" figure needs a
primary or a hedged breakdown (~$106K defense + $53K to the Reporters Committee) — etch no single
dollar figure yet; reconfirm the ~274.5M 2021 figure from a primary or render it ">a quarter";
the ~15 mL revision stays *reported*/primary-pending. **Nothing is published tonight** — this is
a concept brief in the record — so the block binds the first increment; the conductor corrected
the mischaracterization in the record itself so it never travels as fact, which is what clears
the pass. (The reporter's personal name stays citation-only, per the named-individuals policy.)

**Kritiker (blocking, at concept) — OPEN-WORTHY WITH BINDING CONDITIONS.** The material bar
passes clearly: "a specific company financed a specific municipal government's ~13-month legal
effort to withhold a number from its own residents, until a DA overruled it" is a concrete,
nameable act of corporate capture over a local democratic entitlement — stakes a stranger
recognizes without any AI-discourse literacy. The terminal test passes (drink first, no jargon;
the needle-that-won't-settle reads as "it won't give me a straight answer" in seconds), on the
condition the one-line placard is present before the case file. On the takedown law — the brief's
self-written indictment is "a FOIA-request LARP with a fountain" — the Kritiker ruled it
**partially refuted**: Act 1 answers its own charge (a genuine synthetic instrument putting the
field's self-disagreement on one real-time dial; a felt contrast only this machinery produces),
but **Act 2 as first written — a locked box that never opens — free-rides on Act 1 and survives
only because it is *true*, which was never the bar.** Not fatal, but a condition. And it made the
physical-pipeline risk an explicit condition: the studio already carries one unbuilt physical
promise (Recovery), and must not accumulate a second on faith.

## The verdict — OPENED, with binding conditions on increment 1

1. **Screen-first, no fabrication on faith.** Build/gate the browser version first — the
   jittering-needle instrument + the Dalles case — and let it stand *without* the physical
   fountain. If it can't survive without the embodied sip, that is the answer: kill the physical
   extension, don't build it on faith.
2. **State the 70x explicitly; label each dial pin with its model + year.**
3. **Act 2 live, or dropped** — a real, visible submitted-vs-answered counter (a second
   instrument paralleling the needle), or drop the ritual and let the cited case file carry it.
4. **Fact-lock before any publication** — the residents-framing correction; the $153k figure
   sourced or hedged; the 2021 figure reconfirmed or rendered ">a quarter"; the ~15 mL revision
   as *reported*. Every Dalles figure checked against a primary before it is etched/printed as
   fact — the whole moral claim rests on documentary precision aimed at a real company and town.
5. **No fountain/cabinet fabrication scheduled** until the screen increment has passed its gate
   **and** Recovery's fabrication question is resolved one way or the other.

Per the standing precedent, the Kritiker's conditions bind at adoption, not at premiere — they
are part of the brief now. Full record and tiered sourcing: `memory/dossiers/one-tap.md`; the
brief lives at `projects/one-tap/BRIEF.md`.
