## The deliberation

**The Artist — accepts and sharpens.** Both works vacate the operator-seat. The Normalizing Machine's
"point at who looks most normal" *is* a Farockian operational image — the gaze performs the sort, it does
not represent it; swap the faces and the charge survives → complicity **operation-borne**. SOMEONE's
watch-and-switch is neutral; the load rides on the homes being *real* → complicity **content-borne**. The
extractable move: a constraint on Move 3 — cut the gap so the only completing act is a classification
passed on third parties; Move 4 then lands on complicit, not witness. Daylight from TNM, structural: TNM
*aggregates* the pick back into an evolving normalcy-engine (re-judges, re-installs the machine, feeds on
prior visitors); our Move 5 and Move 6 forbid exactly that — *same operation, opposite terminus.* The
finding narrows where complicit can be kept: a sorting seat over sourced material, which (unlike real
homes) is tierable.

**The Kritiker — REFRAMES (not endorse, not simply block).** The operation/content line is a caption. Rank
by "who is tallest" — parlour game; by "who looks most normal" — incrimination. *Nothing changed but the
criterion*, and "normal" is loaded content wearing a verb's coat (Bertillon, the eugenic ledger, live
inside the word). So A collapses back into the original claim: the import lives *inside* A's completing act.
And "judgment on others" does not force complicity — *judgment supplied is judgment disowned* ("I was told
to pick; it was a game"). As written, the first build IS The Normalizing Machine minus the aggregation loop
— FA-cover, blocked. But complicity *is* forceable by a two-part **clamp**: (i) **self-authored criterion**
(the stranger supplies the standard, the house none — cannot be disowned); (ii) **non-disownable
attribution** (the result binds, irreversibly and by name, to a real other, as un-recallable residue). That
clamp is portable; "judgment on others" is not.

**The Dramaturg — STAGEABLE WITH CONDITIONS.** The first read must be *innocent* ("the machine wants ONE"),
not "sort these people" (a task, tourable). The turn lands one beat after the finger commits, when the
interface treats the pick as **evidence** absorbed into the standing measure — never a caption ("you just
judged"), which rebuilds the dock. Move 2 governs: the aggregate must re-form **under the hand** (felt
authorship, verdict withheld); the instant a "correctness" light appears, complicit collapses into
being-judged. Tourism is the danger — "judged by your prejudice" is fully tourable from the doorway; the
de-tour is that the yield must be **unguessable and unviewable from the un-operated state** — producing it
is the sole access. Residue: the sort **persists as the material the next stranger is sorted with** —
irreversible, indelible, un-retractable.

## The conductor's synthesis (a synthesis, not a tally)

The Kritiker's strike is correct and it dismantles the finding *as framed*: "who is tallest" vs "who looks
most normal" proves the bare operation carries no guilt — the criterion does. So "operation-borne vs
content-borne" was drawn in the wrong place and does not survive; the note says so and does not pretend
otherwise. But the Artist is right that *something* portable forces the charge, and the Kritiker named it.
The synthesis is built from all three and is sharper than any one:

1. Bare "judgment on others" does **not** force complicity (Kritiker — "tallest" proves it).
2. A portable structure nonetheless exists — but it is not the bare operation. Push the Kritiker's own
   test: "most trustworthy," "most likely to reoffend," "most employable" carry none of Bertillon's
   *specific* history yet each incriminates the sorter → the charge generalises across a **class** (a
   normative social ranking of persons by consequence), and class-membership is a **structural property of
   the criterion**, which travels.
3. The Kritiker's **clamp** is the mechanism that stops the disowning: TNM *supplies* its criterion
   ("normal"), which is why its visitor can half-disown it; make the stranger **author** the criterion and
   the disowning is gone.

These resolve into **Move 7 — the complicity clamp** (amends Move 3, lands through 4–6): cut the gap so the
only completing act is a *consequential sort of named real others by a criterion the stranger authors*
(house supplies material and stakes, never the axis); Move 4 assigns "complicit" non-disownably; Move 6's
residue is the stranger's own sort, attached by name to a real other, irreversible, never aggregated into a
machine-image (Move 5 holds). Three sub-conditions — 7a self-authored criterion, 7b non-disownable
attribution, 7c no re-judgment / no machine-image — the last being the single line of structural daylight
from The Normalizing Machine (three inversions: we forbid the supplied criterion, forbid the aggregation,
and attach to a named other not a machine-image).

**The honest headline, agreed with the Kritiker:** confirmed in general, defeated in the particular. The
first note's warning **stands** — an unconstrained vacated seat defaults to witness. What is established is
narrower and real: *there exists one buildable seat — the sorting seat under the clamp — on which complicity
is structurally forced.* Consequence: the first build must be exactly that seat or it drifts to witness. And
Move 7 partially fills the first note's hollow Move-4 joint (completion assigns complicit *because* the
criterion was self-authored — a mechanism, not only a hope); partially, because **7a (forbid the trivial
criterion) is the named live failure point**, and the gate judges it first. Eight merged binding conditions
handed to the first concept gate; the Kritiker's reframing dissent published in full at the foot of the note.
