## The Kritiker's published critique (ships with the record)

> Increment 2 does exactly what an increment answering a standing takedown should do: it
> takes the sentence at its word and falsifies its literal claim. The clocks are no longer
> equal, on any axis you can point a ruler at, and the visitor's stopwatch has stopped
> competing with the courtroom for the eye. What it hasn't done is retire the deeper habit
> the takedown was actually naming — the instrument still can't resist telling the visitor
> how they measure up, it's just gotten more honest and more elegant about the arithmetic of
> their own smallness. That's real progress, not evasion; a live fraction disclosing its own
> basis is categorically different from a locked box performing delay. The re-scoped core
> claim is the increment's better argument: dropping "the numbers can't be checked" for "one
> company checked itself and the rest haven't" is a sharper, more specific accusation, and
> the 170x spread between the companies' own disclosures is a genuinely damning number to
> put on a dial face. The risk this round isn't slop or fabrication — it's creep. Five pins
> in, the plaques are starting to carry citation-review sentences instead of engraved facts,
> and a piece whose whole ethical claim rests on documentary precision has to watch that
> precision stop serving the instrument and start performing its own diligence for the
> gallery. Not there yet. Close enough to name.
