## The diagnosis

The conductor's working hypothesis was "staged for a gallery it doesn't have; reads as unfinished;
the thrash reads as a spinner." The **Artist sharpened and partly replaced it** with the deeper
account that governed the restage: **genre-assignment at t=0.** A solitary person at a desk must
decide in two seconds what *kind of thing* they are looking at, and the session-29 opening offered
exactly one available genre — *minimal AI-product page* (near-black theme, a centered input, "Ask it
anything," an animated number on submit). Once filed there, every gesture inverts: the empty field →
loading whitespace; the cycling numeral → a spinner ("computing… please wait"); the tidy five-figure
plate → "it returned five well-cited results." **The refusal reads as its own opposite — a thorough
answer.** And every artistic gesture sat *behind a tap the clean opening gave no reason to perform.*
The old instrument-panel version at least read as "a difficult art object"; the quiet box reads as "a
product, and a boring/broken one" — which is *why* Frank said "even worse." The gate itself had
flagged this ("install dependency," the opening "risks reading as a login screen no one approaches")
and discharged it with a README — and a README is not staging.

The **Dramaturg**, independently, agreed the opening reads as an unfinished/product page and added
the concrete craft notes that survived into the build: cut the malfunction-coded pixel jitter; bond
the visitor's own question to the failing answer; isolate the close; quiet the returned box. Both
voices judged the true form to be the *screen* (under-staged, not medium-mismatched) — with the
Artist's sharp condition: fixable only by *breaking* the session-29 "no auto-run / the tap is the
only crossing" law, and if even that fails the cold desk eye, the concept's body is the physical
install and the screen is a study.

## The decisive move (conductor's synthesis)

The two voices diverged on the single most important decision — the Dramaturg would keep the
interaction-first law and compose a resting mark (a held em-dash); the Artist would break it and open
in the middle of the failure. The conductor took the **Artist's** move: no resting composition of a
search box escapes the product genre at a desk. **Put the refusal in front of the tap.** The piece
now opens already failing — the work poses its own question in serif art-voice, and the answer is
staged as **assertion-and-cancellation**: each published figure asserted as THE reply, held a
readable beat, then STRUCK THROUGH and overwritten by a contradicting one, the strikes accumulating
into a wreckage of five crossed-out named answers that holds none. Redaction, not spinner — and the
strike **rhymes with the Dalles case** (a figure written, then struck, then litigated into secrecy),
so Act 1 and Act 2 become one gesture at two scales, which is what the close has always claimed. The
Dramaturg's essentials were folded in (jitter cut; the tap demoted to optional embodiment — "Ask it
your own question. It will refuse you too."; the close isolated; the returned box quieted).

**One tier-integrity correction of the conductor's own:** the strike belongs to the IMAGINED overture
only; the SOURCED plate stands still and is **never struck** — striking a real figure would imply it
is false, and the figures are all real and irreconcilable. The cardinal rule stays clean: what moves
and cancels is made; what stands still and carries a name is the record.

## The Kritiker's stress-test — and the in-session fixes it forced

The Kritiker earned its seat: it went in to prove the restage still fails and found a **real governing
flaw** in the first build, read straight from the source, not a rubber-stamp. For ~90% of the overture
a clean, bold, magnitude-sized figure sat ON TOP (the live answer) with struck ones demoted below —
which reads as *"best answer + superseded estimates,"* the exact product inversion the restage was
meant to escape; frame f2 showed "0.26 mL / Google" clean on top over five struck lines. And the idle
`setInterval` re-ran the whole refusal to an empty desk — *a literal screensaver loop*, the very
charge session 29 was returned for, contradicting the "runs once" defense. It put ~65–70% odds the eye
still rejects, primary reason = the clean hero. **Both were fixed in-session** (any-change-re-runs):
the answer now strikes each figure *in place*, deduped to five named lines, each clean only for a
brief moving instant — so the resting image is all-struck (verified: **0 of 5 lines live at rest**,
five crossed-out named figures holding none); the screensaver idle-loop was removed entirely (the
refusal runs once on load, is not re-performed to an empty screen); the input was de-producted. Two
residuals the Kritiker named are carried honestly and **published on the work** (README), not hidden:
the still plate reads "five that cancel" more in its caption than its composition (tier-discipline
forbids striking a real figure), and the optional input is still the most product-shaped element.
Re-verified after the fixes: 0 errors, 4/4, byte-identical, 0/5 live at rest.
