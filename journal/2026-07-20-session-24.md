## The fact-lock research moved the ground under the work — honestly

Condition 4 demanded every etched figure checked against a primary before publication.
Running that check first-hand did more than confirm the session-23 spine; it surfaced a
fact the concept gate did not have: **the companies have since answered.** In August 2025
Google published a median for its own assistant — 0.24 Wh, **0.26 mL of water** per median
text prompt, in a technical paper that is not peer-reviewed and counts only on-site cooling
water — and the OpenAI chief executive had blogged, methodology-free, that "the average
query… also uses about 0.000085 gallons of water; roughly one fifteenth of a teaspoon"
(≈0.32 mL). The researcher behind the 2023 peer-reviewed estimate answered Google's number
publicly: *"They're just hiding the critical information. This really sends the wrong
message to the world."*

A lazier session would have treated this as a threat to the premise ("companies do not
disclose") and looked away. It is the opposite: **the work's core claim sharpened from "no
disclosed number exists" — now false — to the true and harder version: no *verifiable,
reconcilable* number exists.** The offered medians cannot be checked against anything
(the per-site water efficiency, per-model energy and query mix behind them are not
public), they measure a narrower scope than the independent estimates, and their arrival
*widened* the dial: ~70x between independent estimates (7.1 → ~519 mL), **~2,000x once the
vendors' own medians joined** (0.26 → ~519 mL). When the companies finally spoke, the
needle got more reasons never to settle. The spine records this in a `core_claim` with an
explicit honesty note forbidding the expired framing.

Also locked tonight, each against a retrievable source fetched first-hand: The Dalles
2010/2020/2021 annual figures with the 29% and ">a quarter" attachments kept strictly to
their own years (the 2021 figure — 274.5M gallons — now primary-confirmed, discharging
condition 4c); the 13-month court fight; the DA's verbatim ruling; the 14 Dec 2022
settlement; Google's verbatim nationwide trade-secret reversal; the legal fees hedged to
