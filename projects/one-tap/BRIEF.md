## What it is
A real public drinking fountain beside a terminal. You drink — one certain, known sip of
real water. You ask the machine a question and watch an analog needle try, and fail, to tell
you how much water your question cost: it never settles, because no disclosed per-query number
exists for it to rest on. Beside it, the documented case of what happened when a whole town
tried to find out its own data-center water use — and had to fight ~13 months in court to pry
loose even the annual figure.

**Subject:** unknowability engineered by power — not "AI is thirsty".

## The spine, in tiers (SOURCED unless marked; full sourcing in the dossier)
- The per-query water figure is publicly estimated anywhere from ~7 mL to ~519 mL — a ~70x
  spread that exists *because* companies do not disclose it. That contested range is the
  instrument, not a defect to smooth over.
- Google's The Dalles, OR data centers drew 355.1M gallons in 2020 = 29% of the city's entire
  water; the city (its legal costs funded by Google) sued the regional newspaper *The Oregonian*
  and its reporter to keep the figures secret; a DA ruled it was not a trade secret; settled
  14 Dec 2022; Google then said it would stop treating site-level water as a trade secret
  nationwide.
